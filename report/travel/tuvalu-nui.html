🌊 Climate change and environmentalism

Main precautions:Seaweed rising, influence of climate change

  • Sea level rise:Tuvalu is reduced in territory due to the rise of sea level due to global warming, limiting access to some regions.
  • Typhoon season:Sudden weather exacerbation during the November-April typhoon season, evacuation plan required
  • Tide change:In the control machine, it is necessary to flood the area and confirm the assistant table
  • Environmental protection:Compliance with strict environmental rules to protect coral reefs and marine ecosystems
🚤 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Limited transportation:You can travel only from Punaputi to small boats to your sister, and stop operating according to weather conditions.
  • Boat safety:Wearing life jackets, prohibit movement when the waves are high
  • Lack of fuel:Fuel supply is irregular, so transportation is delayed
  • Emergency situation:Emergency transfer to Funaputi in the absence of medical staff
  • Communication:Fully blocked communication with the outside when it worsens weather
🏥 Medical and safety precautions
  • Lack of medical facilities:Sisters are only basic medical facilities, so in a serious emergency, Punaputi transfer.
  • Drinking water safety:Relies on rainwater storage tanks, purified water filters or bottled water required
  • Dengue fever attention:Mosquito -mediated diseases risks, mosquito repellents are always used
  • Marine safety:Buddy system required for snorkeling, prohibition of swimming alone
  • Ultraviolet exposure:Strong ultraviolet rays, burns near the equator
💰 Price and living precautions
  • High prices:Price is very high by relying on imports of all items
  • Limited store:Only basic daily necessities can be purchased, necessary items in advance
  • Power instability:Diesel generators' dependence on power outages
  • Internet limit:Satellite on the satellite Internet is slow and expensive
  • Cash payment:Cards cannot be used, enough cash preparation required

💡 Nose travel tip

  • Optimal visits:The dry season in May-October is the best for the weather and sightseeing
  • Homestay Recommendation:Living with a local family and experiencing a true Tuvalu culture
  • Traditional weaving:Traditional weaving technology in Sababe village is a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage Candidate
  • Community contribution:Community -based tourism where tourism revenue is directly delivered to local families
  • Environmental friendly:Prohibition of plastic use, only eco -friendly goods
  • Respect for cultural:Sunday attending church worship, respect for local religious culture
  • Language communication:Mix English and Tuvaluer, learn basic greetings
  • Donation culture:Contributing to the development of community by donating village development funds
