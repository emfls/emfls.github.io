Day 1: Arrival at Sahara Desert and City Exploration

⚠️ El Oued Travel Precautions

🚨 Security and Safety Precautions

Main Areas of Caution: Desert outskirts, late night city areas

  • Foreigner Protection: Algeria is relatively safe as police always monitor for foreigner protection
  • Desert Tour: Always proceed with desert tours with local guides, solo desert entry prohibited
  • Photography: Absolutely no photography of public institutions, police, and military facilities
  • Late Night Outings: Avoid going out alone after 10 p.m. in desert areas
🚌 Transportation and Movement Precautions
  • Domestic Flights: Use domestic flights from Algiers to El Oued, overland travel not recommended
  • Local Transportation: Use taxis or local tour buses, limited public transportation
  • Desert Movement: Must use 4WD vehicles, regular cars cannot enter desert
  • Fuel Check: Limited gas stations in desert areas, ensure sufficient fuel
  • GPS Preparation: Prepare GPS and compass to avoid getting lost in desert
🌡️ Desert Climate Precautions
  • Summer (Jun-Aug): Temperature 40-50°C, extremely hot, recommend early morning or evening activities
  • Winter (Dec-Feb): Day 25°C, night below 5°C, very large temperature difference
  • Sandstorms: Sudden sandstorms may occur, prepare to take shelter indoors
  • Dehydration Risk: Rapid water loss in desert, sufficient water intake essential
  • UV Rays: Strong desert UV rays, SPF 50+ sunscreen essential
💰 Price-related Precautions
  • Cash-based: Limited credit card use, prepare sufficient cash
  • Desert Tour: One-day tour with guide costs $100-200
  • Desert Hotel: $50-150 per night, price varies by facility
  • Food Costs: Local restaurants $10-30, hotel meals more expensive
🕌 Cultural and Religious Precautions
  • Dress Code: Conservative area, recommend wearing long sleeves and long pants
  • Female Travelers: Prepare head scarf, respect local culture
  • Ramadan Period: Refrain from public food consumption during daytime
  • Friday: Islamic prayer day, many shops closed
  • Alcohol: Islamic country with limited alcohol sales

💡 El Oued Travel Tips

  • Best Visiting Period: October-April is the best time for desert travel
  • Local Food: Experience North African traditional foods like couscous, tagine, brik
  • Desert Experience: Special experiences like camel trekking, desert camping, stargazing
  • Local Interaction: El Oued people are very friendly and curious about foreigners
  • Language: Learn basic greetings in Arabic and French
  • Desert Sunrise/Sunset: Must-see spectacular sunrise and sunset in Sahara Desert
  • Traditional Market: Opportunity to purchase local specialties and handicrafts
  • Photography: Desert landscapes and traditional architecture photography spots
