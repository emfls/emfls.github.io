⚠️Sukotai travel precautions

🌡️Checks related to weather

Hot weather:Sukotai is a hot and humid tropical climate of the year, especially the daytime time.

  • High temperature and humidity:If it's too hot, don't come to come, but often relax under the shades of mango trees
  • Strong ultraviolet light:High SPF UV blockers required to prevent burns due to strong ultraviolet rays in the tropics
  • Preparation for the rainy season:Before the rainy season begins, there may be no water in the moat or pond when traveling.
  • Mosquitoism:Due to the nature of the tropics, there are many mosquitoes, so prepare mosquito eradication and long -sleeved clothes
🚴 Precautions when using bicycles
  • Bicycle city:Sukotai is a really good city to travel by bicycle, and most sightseeing is made by bicycle.
  • Safety equipment:Wearing helmets and compliance with safety rules when rental of bicycles
  • Road status:Safe driving required because it moves along the dirt track and irrigation waterway
  • Check the weather:During too hot time, avoid bicycles and recommend using it at sunset.
🏛️ Precautions when watching the History Park
  • visiting hours:Sukotai History Park viewing from 6:30 am to 7:30 pm and 1 hour before the deadline, the ticket inspection is poor.
  • entrance fee:The entrance fee for the History Park must be paid separately and needed to prepare cash.
  • Cultural property protection:It is prohibited to get or touch or touch Buddhas or ruins.
  • Photo shooting:It is possible to sit with a patriarch with the Buddha and leave a commemorative photo, but keep it polite.
🍽️ Food and hygiene precautions
  • Street food:Select a place with a good hygiene when eating local street food
  • Bottled water drinking:Do not drink tap water directly, drink only water or boiled water
  • Iceism:It is safe to avoid the ice of drinks sold on the street
  • Spicy food:Thai food can be very spicy, so you can order it at first
  • Local restaurant:Local food experiences in markets and small restaurants in the center of Old Town
🛕 Religion and cultural courtesy
  • When visiting the temple:Recommended to wear clothes that cover your shoulders and knees, take off your shoes
  • Buddhist shooting:Taking a picture with a Buddha statue is considered a rude behavior.
  • Prohibit the touch of the head:In Thailand, the head is sacred, so it prohibits the touch of others' heads
  • To the toes:It is rude to make the toes to the Buddha or the person
  • Precautionary courtesy:It is common to use gold leaf when you offer offerings to the Buddha.
🚌 Precautions when using transportation
  • Bangkok/Chiang Mai connected:There is a bus to Bangkok and Chiang Mai, so the transportation is not bad
  • Local transportation:It is the most convenient to walk or bicycle in the Old Town
  • Luggage storage:Preparation of bags that can be kept safely when using bicycles
  • Guide Tour:Recommended to use tours with experienced guides speaking English
