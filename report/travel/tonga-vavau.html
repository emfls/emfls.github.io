⚠️ Babau travel precautions

🌊 Marine safetyism

Main precautions:Whale swimming, diving, snorkeling safety rules

  • Whale swimming:Whales and at least 3 meters away from whales, sudden movement prohibition, guide instruction strict compliance
  • Diving Safety:PADI certification confirmation, compliance with birdie system, and safety equipment required for 30 meters of view.
  • Jellyfishism:Advanced jellyfish can be used even in transparent sea, recommended to wear rash guards
  • Algaeism:There is a strong bird section around Babau, prohibiting swimming without a guide
🚤 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Airlines limit:Babau Airport only operates small aircraft,
  • Boat Tour:Cancellation can be canceled according to the weather and the sea situation
  • Rent -a -car:Left pass, road condition, 4WD recommended
  • Taxi fee:No meter, preliminary fee negotiations required
  • Island between islands:Only boats or small aircraft are available, weather -dependent
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Dry (5-10):20-28 degrees, whale season, optimal travel time
  • Woogi (11-4):Temperature 22-30 degrees, wet and cyclone risk
  • ultraviolet rays:The South Pacific strong ultraviolet rays, underwater risk of burns
  • Sudden weather change:Scoles and strong winds of tropical climate
  • Cyclone Season:November-April cyclone can occur
💰 Price related precautions
  • High prices:Most of them are imported 2-3 times as expensive compared to Korea
  • Tour cost:Whale Swimming Tour 200-300 Panga, Diving Tour 150-250 Panga
  • Tip Culture:10-15% tips for guides and boat crews
  • Cash payment:Many small companies receive only cash

💡 Babau Travel Tips

  • Whale season:June-November is the humpback whale observation optimal, especially in August-September
  • Tongga food:Essential experience of Lou (Taro Leaf), Ota Ika (Fish Coconut Milk), and Kaba (Traditional Beverage)
  • Optimal visits:The dry season in May-October is the best for the weather and marine activity
  • Sailing Heaven:Babau is famous as the best sailing area in the South Pacific
  • Polynesian Kingdom:The world's only polynesian kingdom, royal culture can be experienced
  • Diving Mecca:More than 100 feet transparency, 30 or more diving sites
  • Exploration of an uninhabited island:Most of the 61 islands experience uninhabited islands, private beaches
  • Sunday Closed:As a Christian country, most of the shops and tours are suspended on Sunday
