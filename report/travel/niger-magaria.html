⚠️Magaria travel precautions

🚨 security and safetyism

Major areas:Nigerian border areas around the market late at night

  • Border area:Illegal transactions or smuggling activity near the border with Nigeria
  • Night going out:Don't go alone after the sun, accompany you with local guides
  • Photo shooting:Prohibition of shooting military facilities or government buildings
  • Local customs:Respect for Islamic culture, especially Ramadan period
🚌 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Public transportation:Regular bus service restrictions, preliminary schedules required
  • Road status:In the rainy season, the road condition is poor, 4WD car recommended
  • Fuel supply:Gas station limited and sufficient fuel secure
  • Niame connection:It takes about 6-8 hours to the capital
  • Border pass:Passport and visa required when passing the Nigerian border
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Dry (10-5):11-43 degrees, extreme daily crossing and dryness
  • Woogi (June-September):Temperature 22-37 degrees, malaria mosquitoes increased
  • Harmatan:December-February Sahara Desert sand breeze, respiratory attention
  • ultraviolet rays:Strong ultraviolet, UV protection required throughout the year
  • Moisture supplement:High risk of dehydration, enough water intake
🏥 Health -related precautions
  • malaria:Increase in the year -round, especially in the rainy season
  • Water -person disease:Recommended for vaccinations such as cholera and typhoidus
  • Medical facilities:Limited to local medical facilities and measures to prepare for emergency situations
  • drinking water:Only bottled water intake, ice care
  • food:Only eat enough food
