⚠️Chikou and travel precautions

🚨 Health and safetyism

Main risk factors:Malaria, yellow fever, waterborne disease, wildlife

  • malaria:Essentials to be used during the year -round risk area, required to take preventive drugs, and regular use
  • Water quality:Only drinking water, avoid ice, raw fruit and vegetables
  • Wildlife:Do not go out of the car during safari, strict guide instructions
  • Has and crocodiles:Absolute swimming on the Riverside of the Shell, extreme attention when approaching inflation
  • Emergency situation:The nearest medical facility is Blant Tire (50km away)
🚌 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Road status:Many unpaved roads, the possibility of flooding roads during the rainy season
  • 4WD required:Four -wheel drive vehicles required to access wildlife protection zones
  • fuel:There is no gas station in the city of Chiquou and the city.
  • drive:Leave the left side, avoid night driving, animal appearance attention
  • Public transportation:Examination of overload and safety issues when using minibus
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Dry (5-10):The temperature is 15-28 degrees, dry and dusty
  • Woogi (11-4):20-32 degrees, wet and heavy rains
  • Precipitation:An average of 800-1000mm per year, mostly focused on the rainy season
  • ultraviolet rays:Strong sunlight, UV protection required near the equator
  • Daily crossroads:The temperature difference between the day and night is 15 degrees or more
💰 Price related precautions
  • Local currency:Malawi Kwacha (MWK), $ 1 = about 1,600 Kwacha
