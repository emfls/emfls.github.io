⚠️ Bolos travel precautions

🌡️ Mediterranean climate precautions

Summer:The temperature from June-September is high and dry, so enough water intake and UV protection required

  • Prevention of heat stroke:Outdoor activity restraint between 12 pm and 4 pm, rest in the shade
  • Dry climate:Low humidity, risk of dehydration, enough water
  • UV protection:Use high SPF sunscreen, hats and sunglasses are required
  • Winter:December-February is gentle, but it rains a lot of umbrella required
🏔️ Hiking safety by Pelion
  • Hiking trail confirmation:Only the climbing trails marked when mountain climbing in Pelion are used to avoid getting lost.
  • Weather change:In the mountainous areas, the weather changes fast, so prepare for the clothes
  • Safety equipment:Proper hiking boots and backpacks required for hiking
  • Stamina:Sufficient rest in consideration of physical consumption due to high change
🚢 Marine traffic precautions
  • Perry flight:The possibility of cancellation of ferry flights depending on the weather when traveling to islands such as Skiotsu Island
  • Reservation confirmation:In peak season, ferry reservation required, recommendation
  • Preparation for motion sickness:Preparation of motion sickness when traveling
  • Safety rules:Check the location of life jacket when passing by ferry
🏪 Business hours and holidays
  • Sunday Closed:Most shops and markets are closed on Sunday
  • Siesta time:At around 2-5 pm, many shops rest time
  • Museum confirmation:Operating hours and holidays prior confirmation before visiting tourist destinations
  • Felion Train:Seasonal operation schedules are different, so preliminary check
🍽️ Food and drinking water safety

Bolos' food is generally safe, but attention is needed to prevent upset stomach while traveling.

  • Bottled water purchase:Recommended for purchasing bottled water rather than tap water
  • Seafoodism:Choosing fresh seafood, avoid suspicious food
  • Local specialty:Felion San Herbs and Honey are special foods that can be tasted only locally
