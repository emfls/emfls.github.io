⚠️ Namemea travel precautions

🌊 Climate change and environmentalism

Sea level rise:Tuvalu is a direct affected area of sea level rise due to climate change

  • Inundation risk:Temporary flooding during control and storms, weather information preliminary confirmation required
  • Lack of freshwater:Since drinking water supply is limited, water saving and preparation for purification of water purification
  • Coral reef protection:In order to prevent coral reef damage, sunscreen uses Leaf Safe products
  • Garbage processing:All garbage must be taken or processed to a designated place
🚢 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Airlines limit:Only twice a week via Fiji, pre -booking required
  • Island between islands:It is possible to stop operating according to the government's operating boat or charter boat, and the weather
  • Road situation:The pavement is limited and most of the sand roads
  • Public transportation:There are almost no bus or taxi service, walking or using bicycles
  • Emergency situation:Medical staff and emergency services are very limited
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Tropical climate:The temperature is 26-32 degrees throughout the year, higher humidity, higher temperature
  • Woogi (11-4):Recommended to avoid typhoons and heavy rains, avoiding travel plans
  • Dry (5-10):Relatively stable but still showers
  • ultraviolet rays:Ultraviolet rays are very strong near the equator, sufficient sun protection required
  • Marine weather:Restrictions on marine activities due to sudden changes in weather
