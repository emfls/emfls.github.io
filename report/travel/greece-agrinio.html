⚠️Agrinio travel precautions

🌡️ Mediterranean climate precautions

Summer:The temperature from June-September is high and dry, so enough water intake and UV protection required

  • Prevention of heat stroke:Outdoor activity restraint between 12 pm and 4 pm, rest in the shade
  • Dry climate:Low humidity, risk of dehydration, enough water
  • UV protection:Use high SPF sunscreen, hats and sunglasses are required
  • Winter:December-February is gentle, but it rains a lot of umbrella required
🏊 Award -winning safety precautions
  • Lake Triconida:Wearing safety equipment for kayaking and paddle boarding
  • Swimming safety:Check the safety zone when swimming on lakes and beaches
  • Check the weather:Check the weather and wind status before the award activity
  • Life jacket:Recommended for wearing life jackets for all water sports
🚗 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Rent -a -car required:Rent -in -car power is recommended for access to Lake Triconida and nearby tourist attractions
  • Mountain road driving:There is a mountain road to Termo village, so safe driving required
  • Public transportation:Agrinio is a mid -sized city and public transportation is limited
  • Fuel confirmation:The lake area is rarely prepared for fuel because there are rare gas stations.
🏪 Business hours and holidays
  • Sunday Closed:Most shops and markets are closed on Sunday
  • Siesta time:At around 2-5 pm, many shops rest time
  • Museum confirmation:Operating hours and holidays prior confirmation before visiting tourist destinations
  • Culture Festival:Changed some facility operation during the Agrinio Cultural Festival
