⚠️ Taipei travel precautions

🌧️ Monsoon Season

Monsoon Season:In June -September, most of the annual precipitation is concentrated.

  • Woobi and umbrella required:Always portable in preparation for sudden heavy rain
  • Sleeper preparation:Wear instead of sneakers on a rainy day (Tile floor attention)
  • Early time activity:It often rains late in the afternoon, so it starts early at 6-8 am early
  • Indoor activities plan:Preparing indoor schedules such as museums and shopping malls for rainy days
🌪️ Typhoon and earthquake
  • Typhoon alarm:Powerful typhoons in the second half of summer can pass, so check the Central Meteorological Agency
  • Earthquake Response:It is an area where the earthquake occurs frequently.
  • Emergency supplies:Check the emergency evacuation path in advance at the hotel
  • Travel Insurance:Recommended for signing up for travel insurance for natural disasters
🚇 Public transportation etiquette
  • Prohibition of subway:Absolutely prohibited from food, drinks (including water), gum intake
  • Quiet conversation:Do not talk loudly within the subway
  • First of all, concessions:Dark blue priority is for the elderly, pregnant women, and disabled people
  • Easy Card use:Purchase transportation cards available at public transportation and convenience stores
🗣️ Language communication
  • Chinese (Beijing Kwanhwa):It is official language, but can communicate in English in tourist destinations.
  • Basic Chinese:Learn basic greetings such as Nihao (Hello) and Siesi (thank you)
  • Translation app:Installing translation apps such as Google Translator or Papago
  • Tourist Information Center:Korean language guide service to major tourist attractions
🏥 Medical and emergency situations

Taiwan's medical facilities are excellent, but medical expenses can be expensive, so we recommend that you purchase travel insurance. The emergency room operates 24 hours and can communicate with English in major hospitals.
