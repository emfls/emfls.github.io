⚠️San Antonio Travel Note

🚨 security and safetyism

Overall security:San Antonio is a major tourist city in Texas, which is relatively safe, but basic precautions are required.

  • Riverwalk area:Liverwork with a lot of tourists is very safe during the day, but it should be used to light light at night.
  • Downtown area:Alamo and Market Plaza are often police patrols, so they are safe, but remote alleys should be avoided.
  • Tourist destination security:The major tourist attractions are relatively safe because security personnel are arranged.
  • How to deal with:The belongings are always on the body, and it is safe to move to a group at night
🚖 Traffic and airport precautions
  • SAT airport:San Antonio International Airport recommends 13 miles north of downtown, rental car or Uber
  • VIA public transportation:San Antonio's bus system connects major tourist attractions and relatively safety
  • Rent -a -car recommendation:Rent -a -cars are the most convenient when visiting mission tours and suburban tourist destinations
  • Uber/Lift:Easily available and safe in Liverwalk and downtown areas
  • Parking Information:Downtown areas have a lot of paid parking, so check in advance.
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Summer (June-August):Very hot and humid weather can exceed 35 degrees, enough water intake and rest in the shade.
  • Spring/Autumn (March-May, September-November):The best time to travel, the gentle and comfortable weather, the light jacket preparation
  • Winter (12-2):Gentle but sometimes it can be cold, so warm clothes are prepared
  • rainy season:In preparation for umbrellas or waterproofing jackets because it rains a lot in May and September-October
🚫 Airport Security Search Precautions
  • If it is not a TSA approval carrier, the carrier may be torn when searching for security.
  • Thorough luggage test to prevent terrorism, cooperation without friction as much as possible
  • Easily prepare to find when lost by attaching a name tag
