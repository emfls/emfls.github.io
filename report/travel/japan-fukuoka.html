⚠️ Fukuoka travel precautions

🚇 Precautions when using transportation

Fukuoka Tourist City Pass:Transportation such as buses, trains, subways, and ships can be used unlimitedly during the day.

  • Pass fee:2,500 yen of adults in Fukuoka city, 2,800 yen in the city of Fukuoka
  • Qualifications:Limited to foreign travelers who visit Japan for the entry of short -term stays (tourism) from foreign countries
  • Transportation etiquette:Avoid telephone in silent mode and long calls
  • punctuality:In Japan, the time is very important, so compliance with the appointment time
🍽️ Food and cultural precautions
  • Cash use:It is good to exchanged cash enough because the rate of cash use in Japan is high.
  • Tip Culture:Japan has no tip culture, so you don't have to give a tip.
  • Meal etiquette:How to use chopsticks and eat without sound
  • Cultural courtesy:It is important to show respect for local culture
🚭 Precautions when using public places
  • Prohibition of smoking:In many public places in Fukuoka, smoking is prohibited, so use designated smoking zones
  • Garbage processing:In Japan, the separation of garbage is strictly performed, so it must be followed by the separate collection rules.
  • Compliance with Traffic Rules:In Japan, the road traffic law is strict, so the signal must be observed and the pedestrian priority should be followed.
  • Placement:The customs of the restaurant, the lines of clerks, etc. must be well observed.
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Mild climate:Fukuoka has a relatively gentle climate, but there may be a sharp change in weather depending on the season.
  • Preparation of clothes:It is good to prepare flexible clothes for the climate
  • Umbrella preparation:Preparation of umbrella or waterproof jacket for sudden rain
  • UV protection:In preparation for sunlight when the sunlight is strong
🏥 Medical and safety precautions
  • Overseas medical insurance:It is good to have overseas medical insurance in preparation for unexpected accidents during the trip
  • Preparation for emergency situations:Always carry an emergency contact list and accommodation address and contact information
  • Private prescription medicine:If you have any drugs you usually take, prepare enough amount
  • Language communication:Prepare a Japanese conversation book or translation app to prepare for emergencies.
🛍️ Precautions for shopping
  • Tax -free shopping:Duty -free benefits can be made if purchased more than 5,000 yen
  • Receipt:Store receipts so that you can check at the airport when purchasing duty -free products
  • Card use:Credit cards can be used in most stores, but also required to prepare cash.
  • Packaging service:Japan has a very good packaging service, so it is good for purchasing gifts.
