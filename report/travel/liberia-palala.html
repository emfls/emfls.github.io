⚠️Palala travel precautions

🚨 Health and safetyism

Main risk factors:Malaria, yellow fever, drinking water contamination, wild animals

  • malaria:Mosquito repellent required, long -sleeved clothes, mosquito nets, prevention drugs
  • drinking water:Drink only bottled water, avoid ice, and take raw fruit intake
  • Wildlife:Guide order in the national park, thoroughly observed, prohibited behavior alone
  • Emergency situation:Local hospital location in advance, preparation for emergency contact information
🚌 Traffic and mobile precautions
  • Road status:Many unpaved roads, deterioration of road conditions during rainy season
  • Public transportation:Difficulty driving on time, securing enough time to spare time
  • taxi:No meter, preliminary fee negotiations required
  • Rent -a -car:4WD car recommendation, local driving experience needs
  • Night movement:Avoiding as much as possible and inevitable if you are accompanied by locals
🌡️Checks related to weather
  • Woogi (May-October):Rain and flood risks, umbrellas and waterproof products required
  • Dry (11-4):High temperature drying, enough water intake
  • humidity:Wearing high humidity and fast -dried clothing year -round
  • Temperature:An average of 26-30 degrees per year, less
  • ultraviolet rays:Strong ultraviolet, UV protection required near the equator
💰 Price related precautions
  • Cash transaction:Most cash transactions, enough cash preparation
  • Bargaining:Bagoon culture, proper price preliminary survey in the market
  • tip:Guide, Tip Culture for Porter
  • price:Imported goods are expensive and low in local agricultural products
