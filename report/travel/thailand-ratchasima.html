⚠️ Rachashima travel precautions

🌡️Checks related to weather

Tropical climate:Rachashima is a hot and humid tropical climate of the year, especially in summer

  • High temperature and humidity:Summer temperature is very high and humidity is high, so there is a risk of dehydration.
  • Strong ultraviolet light:High SPF UV blockers required to prevent burns due to strong ultraviolet rays in the tropics
  • Preparation for the rainy season:During the rainy season in May-October
  • Mosquitoism:Due to the nature of the tropics, there are many mosquitoes, so prepare mosquito eradication and long -sleeved clothes
🐘 Kaoyai National Park Safety Precautions
  • Wildlife:Maintaining a safe distance with wildlife habitat such as elephants, monkeys and snakes
  • Guide accompanied:Recommended guide to trekking in the national park
  • Designated path:Use only a fixed trekking course and don't go alone
  • Food management:Be careful about the storage of foods that can attract wild animals
  • Waterfall safety:Comply with safety rules when swimming at Hausarok Falls, etc.
🚗 Precautions when using transportation
  • Song Tae -woo use:Rachashima's representative public transportation checks the route and rates in advance
  • Taxi:Be careful when requesting a meter and using it late at night
  • Use Tuk Tuk:Negotiation of the price in advance, wearing seat belts, suitable for short distance movement
  • Rent -a -car:Need for international driver's license, local traffic laws and regulations required
  • Kaoyayi moving:There are many mountain roads, especially when driving,
🍽️ Food and hygiene precautions
  • Street food:Select a place with a good hygiene when eating local street food
  • Bottled water drinking:Do not drink tap water directly, drink only water or boiled water
  • Iceism:It is safe to avoid the ice of drinks sold on the street
  • Spicy food:Foods in discretion can be particularly spicy, so you can order properly at first.
  • Fermented food:Feated foods for discrete areas start with a small amount when you first eat
🛕 Religion and cultural courtesy
  • When visiting the temple:Wearing clothes covering your shoulders and knees, taking off your shoes and entering
  • Buddhist shooting:Taking a picture with a Buddha statue is considered a rude behavior.
  • Prohibit the touch of the head:In Thailand, the head is sacred, so it prohibits the touch of others' heads
  • To the toes:It is rude to make the toes to the Buddha or the person
  • Discrete culture:Rachashima is a separated area that requires respect for cultural characteristics similar to Laos.
🏛️ Precautions when visiting historical sites
  • Pimai History Park:Khmer's ruins do not touch or go up to protect cultural assets
  • Photo shooting:In some ruins, photography may be limited, so check
  • Guide Tour:Recommended to use guided tours to understand the historical background
  • Conservation consciousness:Visitors Etiquette Compliance to preserve historical sites with high historical values
